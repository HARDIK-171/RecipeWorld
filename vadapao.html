Vada Pav Recipe

A popular Indian street food from Maharashtra.

Ingredients

  • For the Vada (Potato Filling):
    • 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
    • 2 green chilies, chopped
    • 10-12 curry leaves
    • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • Salt to taste
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • Coriander leaves, chopped
  • For the Batter:
    • 1 cup chickpea flour (besan)
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
    • Salt to taste
    • Water as needed
  • For Assembling:
    • 4 pav (bread rolls)
    • Green chutney
    • Tamarind chutney
    • Fried green chilies (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Let them splutter.
  2. Once the seeds splutter, add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut√© for a minute.
  3. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well.
  4. Add the mashed potatoes, mix well, and cook for a few minutes. Add lemon juice and chopped coriander leaves. Set aside to cool.
  5. In a bowl, combine chickpea flour, turmeric, chili powder, baking soda, and salt. Add water to form a thick batter.
  6. Shape the potato filling into small balls. Dip each ball into the batter, ensuring it is well coated.
  7. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the coated vadas until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Slice the pav (bread rolls) in half horizontally. Apply green chutney and tamarind chutney on the inside.
  9. Place the vada in the pav and serve hot, optionally garnished with fried green chilies.

Enjoy your homemade Vada Pav!
